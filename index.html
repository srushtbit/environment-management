Water Pollution

What is Water Pollution?
Water pollution is the contamination of water bodies by harmful substances, affecting both wildlife and human health.
Main Causes:
1.Industrial Waste: Factories release untreated chemicals into water.
2.Agricultural Runoff: Pesticides and fertilizers wash into rivers and lakes.
3.Sewage: Untreated wastewater from cities pollutes water sources.
4.Plastic Waste: Single-use plastics harm marine life and water quality.
Effects:
1.Ecosystem Damage: Harmful to aquatic plants and animals.
2.Health Risks: Contaminated water can cause diseases.
3.Economic Impact: Affects fisheries, tourism, and local economies.
What Can We Do?
Reduce Plastic Use: Choose reusable over single-use plastics.
Safe Disposal: Properly dispose of chemicals and waste.
Support Clean Water Initiatives: Join or support organizations working to clean and protect water sources.
Take Action! Educate yourself and others about water pollution. Small changes can make a big difference!
